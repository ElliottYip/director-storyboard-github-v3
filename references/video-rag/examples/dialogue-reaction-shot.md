# Dialogue Reaction Shot

Use for conversation, listening, argument, confession, shot/reverse-shot logic, two-person tension, or silent confrontation.

## Purpose

The shot should preserve spatial logic and make speaking/listening behavior clear without identity blending.

## Required Prompt Details

- Bind positions: left/right, foreground/background, seated/standing, distance between characters.
- Identify who speaks, who listens, and whether mouth movement is needed.
- If no dialogue should be generated, explicitly write no dialogue / closed mouth / listening only.
- Respect eyelines and axis: do not flip screen direction unless the cut intentionally crosses the line.
- Use one camera idea: over-shoulder, two-shot, profile two-shot, reaction close-up, or locked medium.
- End on a reaction, silence, gaze hold, or cuttable pause.

## Avoid

- Two people speaking at once unless intended.
- Face or body blending between characters.
- Unmotivated angle changes inside one generated clip.
- Vague "they talk emotionally" phrasing.

## Useful Structure

```text
00:00-00:02 establish who is speaking and who is listening.
00:02-00:06 speaker delivers / listener reacts with micro-expression.
00:06-00:10 pause lands; eyeline and body distance remain stable.
结尾：停在听者或说话者的明确反应上。
```
