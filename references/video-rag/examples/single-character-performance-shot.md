# Single Character Performance Shot

Use for realization, grief, suspicion, restraint, decision, inner conflict, close-up reactions, or a character holding ground.

## Purpose

The shot should make one internal change readable through eyes, breath, posture, and tiny physical signals.

## Required Prompt Details

- Name the character's start pose, gaze direction, emotional restraint, and physical state.
- Use micro-actions: blink timing, swallow, breath, jaw tension, hand pressure, shoulder drop.
- Keep mouth behavior explicit: no dialogue, whispered line, closed lips, breath only, or synced speech.
- Camera should support performance: static close-up, slow push-in, slight profile drift, or locked medium shot.
- Face integrity must be protected from shadow crushing, distortion, over-sharpening, or mask-like skin.
- End on a readable held expression for 1 second.

## Avoid

- Big emotion words without visible behavior.
- Sudden crying, screaming, kneeling, or collapse unless explicitly requested.
- Camera motion that distracts from the face.
- Over-darkening the face to chase mood.

## Useful Structure

```text
00:00-00:02 hold start pose and eyeline.
00:02-00:05 one micro-reaction begins.
00:05-00:08 breath / swallow / gaze shift reveals the decision.
结尾：表情停住，眼神保持方向，形成可剪切静帧。
```
