# Establishing Location Shot

Use for the first shot of a scene, empty plates, geography, time-of-day, weather, mood, or spatial orientation.

## Purpose

The shot must tell the model where the scene is, how space is organized, and what visual rules matter before characters or action dominate.

## Required Prompt Details

- State interior/exterior, time of day, weather, foreground/midground/background.
- Define the main light direction and what it touches first.
- Name one dominant material surface: stone, wood, glass, fabric, metal, water, dust, paper.
- Keep the camera movement simple: static, slow push, slow lateral reveal, or gentle tilt.
- If characters appear, keep them secondary unless the shot is also an entrance.
- End on a cuttable stable frame: doorway visible, table revealed, corridor empty, crowd in position, or light source held.

## Avoid

- Abstract mood without geography.
- Multiple camera moves in one clip.
- Overdescribing objects that will not matter later.
- Readable random text on signs, papers, screens, or walls.

## Useful Structure

```text
00:00-00:02 establish foreground / light / weather.
00:02-00:06 reveal spatial relationship.
00:06-00:10 hold the key entrance route, object, or empty space.
结尾：画面稳定停在可剪切构图。
```
